About This Tool

This tool is designed to assist with the generation of bibliographic records in MARC21 format with the use of an LLM. Not all give the same quality records. You will need to test which would work best for you.

This tool does not teach cataloguing, so it is important to have a basic understanding of the principles of cataloguing and metadata creation.

The tool is designed around the use of OCLC's API and Record Manager. You can still use it to generate the prompts for your AI/LLM and other system, though you might have to adjust the prompt file, which can be found inside handlers/prompt.py

How to Use: The Cataloging Workflow
1

Prepare Metadata: Gather all necessary text to catalogue. Use a proper, reliable source such as the official publishing website for the description and Table of Contents.

Where to find good source material:

  • Publisher's official site – Most reliable for description, TOC, and trim size.
  • Library catalog records (e.g., Library of Congress, WorldCat) – Great for standardized subject headings and MARC data.
  • Bookseller listings (AbeBooks, Alibris) – Useful for condition notes, special markings, and edition details.
  • Author's website – Often has summaries and awards info not found elsewhere.

What to collect before pasting into the "Book Information / Metadata" field:

  • Title, subtitle, author(s), editor(s), translator(s)
  • Edition (e.g., "First edition, 2nd printing" or "Revised paperback")
  • ISBN (10 or 13 digits), publisher, publication date
  • Physical details: page count, height/dimensions, binding type
  • Official description or back-cover blurb
  • Table of Contents (especially for non-fiction or scholarly works)
  • Special markings: inscriptions, bookplates, signatures, ex-library stamps

Tip: Don't worry about formatting perfectly. The AI can extract structured metadata from messy copy-paste, including plain text, bullet points, or even scanned OCR snippets. When in doubt, paste more rather than less — it's better to have extra info than to miss a key detail.

2

Generate Prompt: Enter the details into the Catalog tab and click "Generate Prompt." The structured MARC21 instructions will be automatically copied to your clipboard.

3

AI Processing: Paste the prompt from your clipboard into your preferred AI (Claude, Gemini, or ChatGPT). The AI will generate the raw MARCXML code based on the strict constraints provided.

4

Validate & Submit: Copy the MARCXML provided by the AI and paste it back into the "MARCXML Output" box in this tool. Review the data for any errors (use the validator as much as possible), then click "Submit to WorldCat."

Security & Encryption

To protect your institution's API secrets, this application encrypts your Client ID and Secret before saving them to disk using Fernet (AES-128) symmetric encryption.

⚠️ Critical: Changing the Encryption Key

If you wish to change the security key, you must do so before saving any credentials in the Configuration tab. If you change the key after saving credentials, the app will no longer be able to read your old credentials.

To change the key, open handlers/config.py and modify the SECRET_KEY variable:

In the terminal:
python -c "from cryptography.fernet import Fernet; print(Fernet.generate_key())"
Then copy the key here:
SECRET_KEY = b'Your-New-32-Byte-Base64-Key='
Disclaimer: AI Usage & Data Accuracy

Workflow Notice: This tool generates a structured prompt designed to be copied and processed by your preferred AI (e.g., Claude, Gemini, ChatGPT). The application does not generate the MARC record directly.

  • AI Limitations: Even with strict constraints and instructions provided in the prompt to prevent "hallucinations," AI models may still produce incorrect formatting, invalid subject headings, or non-existent URIs.
  • Mandatory Review: It is the user's responsibility to review the AI's output for accuracy and MARC21 compliance before pasting it back into this tool for OCLC submission.
  • No Direct Connection: This application has no control over the privacy policies or the internal logic of the external AI service you choose to use.

Note: While the prompt is optimized to minimize errors, the AI's response is probabilistic. The cataloger remains the final authority on all metadata submitted to WorldCat.
